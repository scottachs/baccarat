The Goal of Baccarat

Two hands are dealt each round: Player and Banker. The hand closest to a total of 9 wins. Guests bet on which hand will win, or if the result will be a Tie. As the dealer, your job is to deal the cards, apply the drawing rules, and handle payouts.

How Card Totals Work

Add up the card values and only the last digit counts. For example, 7 + 8 = 15, so the hand total is 5. The highest possible total is 9.

Card Values

Ace = 1
2-9 = Face Value
10, J, Q, K = 0

Examples

7+5=12 → 2
0+9 = Natural 9
6+4=10 → 0

Why Player Rules Matter

As the dealer, the Player hand is always acted on first. You must know immediately whether to draw a third card, stand, or announce a Natural. Getting this wrong delays the game and can lead to dealing errors. The Player rules are simpler than Banker rules, so mastering them first builds a strong foundation.

Naturals: 8 or 9

If either the Player or Banker has 8 or 9 on their first two cards, it is called a Natural. The hand is over immediately and no more cards are drawn for either side. As the dealer, you announce the Natural and proceed directly to determining the winner.

8 or 9 on first two cards = NATURAL. No more cards for either hand.

Player Standing Rule: 6 or 7

If the Player total is 6 or 7, the Player stands. No third card is drawn. This is important because when the Player stands, the Banker follows a simpler set of rules for their own third card decision.

Player has 6 or 7 = PLAYER STANDS. No third card.

Player Drawing Rule: 0 through 5

If the Player total is 0 through 5, the Player must draw a third card. This third card is dealt face up and its value becomes critical for determining what the Banker does next.

Player has 0 through 5 = PLAYER DRAWS a third card.

Quick Reference

8 or 9Natural. Hand is over. No more cards.
6 or 7Player stands. No third card drawn.
0 through 5Player draws a third card.

Remember

The simple way to remember: 5 and under always draws. 6 and 7 always stands. 8 and 9 is a Natural. As a dealer, always check both hands, Player and Banker, for Naturals first before deciding whether the Player draws.
